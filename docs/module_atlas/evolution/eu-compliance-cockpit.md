## 9 · Future Evolution

### 9.1 Evolution A — Persist cockpit runs and give the scorecard a real methodology (analytics ladder: rung 2 → 3)

**What.** This is one of the platform's genuinely wired showcases: one company profile walked through **five live backend engines** — taxonomy Article 3 test, EUDR due diligence, ETS2 readiness, EuGB issuance assessment, iXBRL export — with a real cross-step dependency (Step 1's DNSH/minimum-safeguards results feed Step 4's EuGB assessment). The gaps are at the edges: nothing persists (a full compliance walkthrough evaporates on refresh), the Step 6 scorecard is a local aggregation with ad-hoc weights (`0.5·sc + 0.3·dnshPct + 0.2·msScore`) that no engine owns, and coverage is bounded by the 8-option NACE list and hand-authored TSC table upstream.

**How.** (1) `compliance_cockpit_runs` table storing the profile and all five engine responses per run, so compliance posture is trackable across reporting years and auditable — the cockpit becomes a record, not a demo. (2) Promote the scorecard to a backend endpoint with documented weighting rationale (or replace the composite with per-regulation RAG statuses — a single blended "EU compliance score" across five unrelated regulations is methodologically dubious; the honest design may be no composite at all, stated explicitly). (3) Coverage rides upstream improvements: the eu-taxonomy engine's NACE/TSC expansion (its own §9.1) automatically widens the cockpit's Step 1. (4) Rung 3: pin the five-step chain as an end-to-end integration test with a golden profile — this module is the platform's best candidate for a full-stack regression fixture.

**Prerequisites.** The hard-coded `API = localhost:8001` moved to env config; Alembic migration. **Acceptance:** a saved run reloads with all five engine responses intact; the golden-profile integration test passes in CI; the scorecard either cites a documented methodology or has been replaced by per-regulation statuses.

### 9.2 Evolution B — Conversational EU-compliance orchestrator (LLM tier 3)

**What.** The cockpit is already a hard-coded tier-3 orchestration — five engines, one workflow. Evolution B makes it conversational and adaptive: "we're a mid-cap cement producer entering the EU market — what applies to us and where do we stand?" The orchestrator determines applicability (taxonomy eligibility by NACE, EUDR by commodity footprint, ETS2 by fuel use, EuGB only if issuing), runs the *relevant* subset of the five engines with the user's profile, and returns a compliance memo — skipping inapplicable steps with the reason stated, rather than marching every company through all five.

**How.** Tool surface = the five engines' existing endpoints (all live and Pydantic-typed — this module needs zero new backend for tier 3, uniquely in this slice); routing logic from each regulation's applicability rules, encoded as checkable predicates rather than LLM judgment where the rules are crisp (ETS2 fuel-type triggers), with LLM judgment reserved for genuinely fuzzy scoping (and labeled as such). The memo carries the per-step engine verdicts with regulation-article citations the engines already return; the iXBRL export step remains a gated action.

**Prerequisites (hard).** Evolution A's persistence (a conversational run must land in the same auditable record); applicability predicates reviewed against the regulations. **Acceptance:** a golden cement-producer query runs exactly the applicable engine subset with skip-reasons for the rest; every verdict in the memo matches its engine response; the same conversation re-run produces the same routing (determinism where rules are crisp).
