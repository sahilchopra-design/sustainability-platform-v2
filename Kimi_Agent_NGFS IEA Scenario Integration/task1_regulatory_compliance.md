## 1. NGFS Phase 4/5 Compliance Requirements

### 1.1 Overview and Evolution

The Network for Greening the Financial System (NGFS) has published multiple vintages of climate scenarios since 2020. The **Phase V scenarios** (published November 2024) represent the most current framework, superseding Phase IV with significant enhancements to physical risk modeling and scenario architecture.

**Key Regulatory Citations:**
- NGFS Climate Scenarios for Central Banks and Supervisors - Phase V (November 2024)
- NGFS Climate Scenarios Technical Documentation (January 2025)
- NGFS Guide to Climate Scenario Analysis for Central Banks and Supervisors (2024 Update)
- NGFS Short-term Scenarios (2025) - First vintage with 3-5 year horizons

### 1.2 Scenario Selection Criteria Per Jurisdiction

#### 1.2.1 Core Scenario Categories

The NGFS scenarios are organized into three primary transition pathways:

| Scenario Category | Description | Temperature Outcome |
|-------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Orderly Transition** | Early, ambitious climate policies; smooth transition | 1.5°C - 2°C |
| **Disorderly Transition** | Delayed or divergent policies; abrupt adjustments | 1.5°C - 2°C (higher costs) |
| **Hot House World** | No additional climate policies; physical risks dominate | >3°C |

#### 1.2.2 Phase V Scenario Suite (Seven Scenarios)

1. **Net Zero 2050 (Orderly)** - Limit warming to 1.5°C through immediate, coordinated global action
2. **Below 2°C (Orderly)** - Limit warming to below 2°C with immediate action
3. **NDCs (Disorderly)** - Policies to achieve Nationally Determined Contributions only
4. **Current Policies (Hot House World)** - No additional climate policies beyond those currently implemented
5. **Delayed Transition (Disorderly)** - Coordinated global action begins in 2030
6. **Fragmented World (Disorderly)** - Uncoordinated regional policies with trade tensions
7. **Low Demand (Orderly)** - Rapid shift away from fossil fuels due to demand-side changes

#### 1.2.3 Jurisdiction-Specific Adaptation Requirements

| Jurisdiction | Scenario Selection Mandate | Adaptation Requirements |
|--------------|---------------------------|------------------------|
| **European Union** | Minimum 3 scenarios (Orderly, Disorderly, Hot House World) | Align with EU Green Deal and Fit for 55 targets |
| **United Kingdom** | At least 2 transition scenarios + Current Policies | Reflect UK net zero 2050 commitment |
| **United States** | Scenario selection based on materiality assessment | Consider state-level policy variations |
| **Canada** | Align with OSFI B-15 scenario requirements | Reflect Canadian 2030 emissions reduction target |
| **Australia** | Minimum 2 climate scenarios | Consider regional physical risk variations |
| **Japan** | NGFS scenarios as baseline | Integrate with Japan's GX (Green Transformation) strategy |

### 1.3 Time Horizon Requirements

#### 1.3.1 Three-Tier Time Horizon Framework

| Horizon Type | Duration | Primary Use Case | Regulatory Requirement |
|--------------|----------|------------------|------------------------|
| **Short-term** | 3-5 years | Climate Stress Testing (CST) | Mandatory from 2027 (EBA GL/2025/04) |
| **Medium-term** | 10 years | Capital planning, risk appetite | Required for ICAAP/ORSA |
| **Long-term** | 30-100 years (to 2100) | Strategic resilience, transition planning | Climate Resilience Analysis (CRA) |

#### 1.3.2 Short-Term Scenarios (New in 2025)

The NGFS published its first vintage of short-term scenarios in 2025 with the following characteristics:

- **Time horizon:** Until 2030
- **Scenario types:**
  - Policy shock scenario (carbon price increases)
  - Technology shock scenario (rapid clean technology adoption)
  - Double shock scenario (combined policy + technology)
  - Confidence shock scenario (consumer/business confidence drop)

**Key Variables for Short-Term Scenarios:**
- GDP impact projections
- Carbon price trajectories
- Energy price shocks
- Critical mineral supply bottlenecks
- Regional divergence effects

#### 1.3.3 Long-Term Scenario Specifications (Phase V)

**Updated Parameters:**
- SSP 3.0 (Shared Socioeconomic Pathways) - released January 2024
- Population projections: 9.6 billion by 2050 (200 million higher than Phase IV)
- GDP growth: 3.4% annually to mid-century (lower than Phase IV)
- New damage function incorporating:
  - Average annual temperature
  - Daily temperature variability
  - Total annual precipitation
  - Number of wet days
  - Extreme daily rainfall
  - Lagged effects up to 10 years post-shock

### 1.4 Variable Coverage Mandates

#### 1.4.1 Mandatory Macroeconomic Variables

| Variable Category | Specific Variables | Frequency |
|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Economic Output** | GDP (nominal and real), sectoral GDP | Annual |
| **Price Levels** | CPI, energy prices, carbon prices | Annual |
| **Labor Markets** | Employment, unemployment rates | Annual |
| **Financial Markets** | Equity indices, bond yields, risk premia | Annual |
| **Energy Systems** | Fossil fuel consumption, renewable capacity | Annual |
| **Emissions** | CO2, CH4, N2O by sector and region | Annual |

#### 1.4.2 Physical Risk Variables (Enhanced in Phase V)

| Variable Type | Metrics | Application |
|---------------|---------|-------------|
| **Acute Physical Risk** | Extreme weather event frequency/severity | Loan-level PD/LGD adjustments |
| **Chronic Physical Risk** | Sea level rise, temperature increases | Collateral valuation adjustments |
| **Agricultural Impact** | Crop yield changes, water stress | Sectoral risk assessments |
| **Infrastructure Risk** | Damage functions by asset type | Real estate exposure analysis |

#### 1.4.3 Transition Risk Variables

| Variable Type | Metrics | Application |
|---------------|---------|-------------|
| **Policy Risk** | Carbon price trajectories, policy stringency indices | Corporate credit risk |
| **Technology Risk** | Clean technology adoption rates, stranded asset values | Sectoral concentration risk |
| **Market Sentiment** | ESG investment flows, green premium/discount | Market risk assessments |
| **Legal/Regulatory** | Litigation risk indicators, compliance costs | Operational risk |

### 1.5 Disclosure Requirements by Scenario

#### 1.5.1 Minimum Disclosure Standards

| Disclosure Element | Requirement Level | Timeline |
|-------------------|-------------------|----------|
| Scenario narratives | Mandatory - qualitative description | Immediate |
| Key assumptions | Mandatory - documented and justified | Immediate |
| Variable trajectories | Mandatory - quantitative where available | Immediate |
| Sensitivity analysis | Recommended - alternative assumptions | 2025+ |
| Model documentation | Mandatory - methodology transparency | Immediate |

#### 1.5.2 Scenario-Specific Disclosure Requirements

| Scenario Type | Specific Disclosures |
|---------------|---------------------|
| **Orderly Transition** | Policy coordination assumptions, technology deployment rates, just transition measures |
| **Disorderly Transition** | Delay rationale, abrupt adjustment mechanisms, financial stability impacts |
| **Hot House World** | Physical risk accumulation assumptions, adaptation capacity limits, tipping points |

### 1.6 Cross-Border Consistency Rules

#### 1.6.1 Harmonization Principles

1. **Common Scenario Baseline:** All jurisdictions must use NGFS scenarios as the starting point for analysis
2. **Consistent Variable Definitions:** Standardized definitions for key risk drivers across jurisdictions
3. **Comparable Time Horizons:** Alignment on short (3-5 years), medium (10 years), and long-term (30+ years) horizons
4. **Shared Assumptions:** Common SSPs, RCPs, and IAM calibrations

#### 1.6.2 Cross-Border Reporting Requirements

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Scenario Mapping** | Document how local scenarios map to NGFS reference scenarios |
| **Divergence Justification** | Explain any material deviations from NGFS baseline assumptions |
| **Cross-Border Exposures** | Apply consistent scenarios to internationally diversified portfolios |
| **Currency Harmonization** | Convert all monetary variables to common reporting currency |

#### 1.6.3 Data Sharing Protocols

- **Granularity:** Share scenario outputs at country and sector level where possible
- **Frequency:** Annual updates with interim revisions for major policy changes
- **Quality Standards:** Document data sources, validation procedures, and uncertainty ranges

## 2. Basel IV Climate Risk Integration

### 2.1 Overview

Basel IV (implemented in EU from January 1, 2025) introduces comprehensive reforms to the Basel III framework with explicit integration of climate-related financial risks. The 2024 revisions to the Basel Core Principles (BCP) formalize climate risk expectations across all three pillars.

**Key Regulatory Citations:**
- Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS) Core Principles for Effective Banking Supervision - 2024 Revision
- BCBS Principles for the Effective Management and Supervision of Climate-Related Financial Risks (June 2022)
- CRR III/CRD VI (EU implementation of Basel IV)

### 2.2 Pillar 1: Minimum Capital Requirements Adjustments

#### 2.2.1 Standardized Approach (SA) Modifications

| Risk Category | Climate Risk Integration | Effective Date |
|---------------|-------------------------|----------------|
| **Credit Risk SA** | Enhanced sectoral risk weights for carbon-intensive exposures | January 2025 |
| **Real Estate Risk** | Location-based risk adjustments for physical risk exposure | January 2025 |
| **CVA Risk** | Climate-adjusted counterparty credit risk | January 2025 |
| **Operational Risk** | New standardized approach incorporating climate-related operational risks | January 2025 |

#### 2.2.2 Credit Risk Standardized Approach Enhancements

**New Asset Classes with Climate Considerations:**

| Asset Class | Climate Risk Treatment |
|-------------|----------------------|
| **Corporate Exposures** | Sectoral multipliers for high-emitting industries |
| **Project Finance** | Enhanced due diligence for fossil fuel projects |
| **Real Estate** | Physical risk overlays for flood/fire-prone regions |
| **Sovereign** | Climate vulnerability assessments for emerging markets |

#### 2.2.3 Output Floor Implications for Climate Risk

| Output Floor Parameter | Specification | Climate Risk Consideration |
|-----------------------|---------------|---------------------------|
| **Floor Level** | 72.5% (fully phased in by 2030) | Ensures minimum capital for climate-exposed portfolios |
| **Phase-in Schedule** | 50% (2025) → 72.5% (2030) | Gradual adjustment for climate risk model development |
| **Calculation Base** | Standardized Approach RWA | Climate risk adjustments included in SA calculations |
| **Transitional Arrangements** | Until end of 2032 | Allows for climate risk methodology refinement |

**Impact on Climate Risk Modeling:**
- Limits capital benefit from internal models for climate risk
- Encourages development of robust standardized approaches
- Reduces variability in climate risk capital calculations
- Enhances comparability across institutions

### 2.3 Pillar 2: ICAAP Climate Risk Add-ons

#### 2.3.1 ICAAP Climate Risk Integration Requirements

Per BCBS Principle 5 and ECB expectations, banks must:

| Requirement | Description | Implementation Timeline |
|-------------|-------------|------------------------|
| **Materiality Assessment** | Identify and assess material climate-related financial risks | Immediate |
| **Quantification** | Develop processes to evaluate solvency impact of climate risks | Progressive |
| **Capital Planning** | Incorporate climate risks into capital planning horizons | By 2027 |
| **Risk Aggregation** | Include climate risks in overall ICAAP risk aggregation | Progressive |

#### 2.3.2 Climate Risk Add-on Calculation Methodologies

**Methodology 1: Scenario-Based Add-on**

```
Climate Risk Add-on = Σ (Scenario Impact_i × Probability_i × Time Weight_i)
```

Where:
- Scenario Impact_i = Capital impact under scenario i
- Probability_i = Assigned probability of scenario i
- Time Weight_i = Discount factor for time horizon

**Methodology 2: Stress Testing Buffer**

| Component | Calculation Approach |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Transition Risk Buffer** | Difference between orderly and disorderly scenario capital requirements |
| **Physical Risk Buffer** | Expected loss under severe physical risk scenarios |
| **Combined Buffer** | Integrated assessment considering correlation effects |

**Methodology 3: Expert Judgment Overlay**

| Risk Category | Expert Judgment Application |
|---------------|---------------------------|
| **Emerging Risks** | Qualitative assessment for nascent climate risks |
| **Data Limitations** | Conservative overlays where data is insufficient |
| **Model Uncertainty** | Model risk premium for climate risk models |

#### 2.3.3 ICAAP Documentation Requirements

| Document Element | Required Content |
|-----------------|------------------|
| **Climate Risk Inventory** | Comprehensive list of material climate risks |
| **Materiality Assessment** | Methodology and results of materiality determination |
| **Scenario Analysis** | Description of scenarios used and key assumptions |
| **Capital Impact Assessment** | Quantified impact on capital adequacy |
| **Management Actions** | Planned responses to climate risk findings |
| **Validation** | Independent review of climate risk ICAAP components |

### 2.4 Pillar 3: Disclosure Requirements

#### 2.4.1 Pillar 3 Climate Risk Disclosures

| Disclosure Category | Requirements | Frequency |
|--------------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Qualitative Disclosures** | Governance, strategy, risk management approaches | Annual |
| **Quantitative Disclosures** | Exposures, metrics, targets | Semi-annual |
| **Scenario Analysis** | Results and key assumptions | Annual |
| **Transition Planning** | Progress towards climate targets | Annual |

#### 2.4.2 Green Asset Ratio (GAR) and Banking Book Taxonomy Alignment Ratio (BTAR)

| Metric | Definition | Reporting Start |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **GAR** | % of taxonomy-aligned assets to total covered assets | 2024 (2023 data) |
| **BTAR** | % of taxonomy-aligned banking book to total banking book | Voluntary |

**GAR Calculation Components:**
- Taxonomy-eligible economic activities
- Technical screening criteria compliance
- Do No Significant Harm (DNSH) assessment
- Minimum safeguards compliance

#### 2.4.3 Physical Risk Disclosure Requirements

| Disclosure Element | Specification |
|-------------------|---------------|
| **Exposure Mapping** | Geographic and sectoral breakdown of physical risk exposures |
| **Risk Metrics** | Quantified physical risk metrics (e.g., expected annual damage) |
| **Scenario Results** | Physical risk impacts under NGFS scenarios |
| **Mitigation Measures** | Insurance coverage, collateral requirements, risk transfer |

### 2.5 Internal Ratings-Based (IRB) Adjustments

#### 2.5.1 IRB Climate Risk Integration Requirements

Per Article 177(2a) CRR, institutions using IRB must:

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **PD Adjustments** | Incorporate climate risk drivers into probability of default estimates |
| **LGD Adjustments** | Reflect climate impacts on loss given default |
| **EAD Adjustments** | Consider climate risks in exposure at default calculations |
| **Maturity Adjustments** | Account for climate risk over loan maturities |

#### 2.5.2 Climate Risk Parameter Estimation

| Parameter | Climate Risk Integration Approach |
|-----------|----------------------------------|
| **PD** | Climate-adjusted default probabilities using sectoral and geographic overlays |
| **LGD** | Physical risk impacts on collateral values; transition risk impacts on recovery rates |
| **Correlation** | Sectoral correlation adjustments for climate-sensitive industries |

#### 2.5.3 IRB Model Validation for Climate Risk

| Validation Element | Requirements |
|-------------------|--------------|
| **Backtesting** | Compare climate-adjusted predictions with actual outcomes |
| **Benchmarking** | Compare internal estimates with external climate risk models |
| **Sensitivity Analysis** | Test parameter sensitivity to climate risk assumptions |
| **Documentation** | Document climate risk adjustments to IRB parameters |

## 3. OSFI B-15 Guideline Compliance

### 3.1 Overview

OSFI Guideline B-15: Climate Risk Management establishes mandatory climate-related financial disclosure expectations for Federally Regulated Financial Institutions (FRFIs) in Canada. The guideline is structured around the four pillars of the TCFD framework.

**Key Regulatory Citations:**
- OSFI Guideline B-15: Climate Risk Management (March 7, 2025)
- OSFI Letter to Industry - Updating Guideline B-15 for CSSB Standards (February 20, 2025)
- CSSB Standards (December 18, 2024)

### 3.2 Climate Risk Governance Requirements

#### 3.2.1 Board Responsibilities

| Responsibility | OSFI Expectation |
|----------------|------------------|
| **Oversight** | Board must oversee climate risk management as part of overall risk governance |
| **Strategy Integration** | Climate risks must be integrated into business strategy discussions |
| **Risk Appetite** | Board must approve climate risk appetite statement |
| **Resources** | Ensure adequate resources for climate risk management |
| **Accountability** | Clear assignment of climate risk responsibilities to Senior Management Functions |

#### 3.2.2 Senior Management Responsibilities

| Function | Climate Risk Responsibilities |
|----------|------------------------------|
| **Chief Risk Officer** | Overall climate risk framework design and implementation |
| **Chief Financial Officer** | Climate risk financial impacts and disclosure |
| **Chief Executive Officer** | Strategic integration of climate risk considerations |
| **Climate Risk Officer** | Day-to-day climate risk management (if designated) |

#### 3.2.3 Three Lines of Defense Model

| Line | Climate Risk Responsibilities |
|------|------------------------------|
| **First Line** | Business units identify and manage climate risks in their activities |
| **Second Line** | Risk management function oversees climate risk framework |
| **Third Line** | Internal audit provides independent assurance on climate risk controls |

### 3.3 Scenario Analysis Mandates

#### 3.3.1 Scenario Analysis Requirements

| Requirement | OSFI Specification |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Scenario Types** | Must include 1.5°C and 2°C transition pathways |
| **Time Horizons** | Short, medium, and long-term assessments |
| **Risk Categories** | Both physical and transition risks |
| **Resilience Testing** | Assess business model resilience under different scenarios |
| **Frequency** | At least annually, or more frequently if material changes |

#### 3.3.2 Scenario Selection Criteria

| Criterion | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Materiality** | Scenarios must address material climate risks |
| **Plausibility** | Scenarios must be economically and physically plausible |
| **Range** | Must cover range of outcomes (orderly to disorderly) |
| **Consistency** | Scenarios must be internally consistent |

#### 3.3.3 Use of Scenario Analysis Results

| Application | OSFI Expectation |
|-------------|------------------|
| **Strategy** | Inform strategic planning and business model adjustments |
| **Risk Appetite** | Set and adjust climate risk appetite |
| **Capital Planning** | Consider in ICAAP and capital adequacy assessment |
| **Disclosure** | Disclose key findings and management responses |

### 3.4 Disclosure Expectations

#### 3.4.1 Disclosure Timeline

| Disclosure Element | Implementation Date |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Governance, Strategy, Risk Management** | Fiscal periods ending on or after October 1, 2024 |
| **Scope 1 and 2 GHG Emissions** | Fiscal periods ending on or after October 1, 2024 |
| **Scope 3 GHG Emissions** | Fiscal year 2028 (revised from 2025) |
| **Scope 3 for Off-Balance Sheet AUM** | Fiscal year 2029 |

#### 3.4.2 TCFD-Aligned Disclosure Framework

| TCFD Pillar | OSFI Disclosure Requirements |
|-------------|------------------------------|
| **Governance** | Board and senior management oversight; accountability structures |
| **Strategy** | Climate risks/opportunities; business impact; resilience testing |
| **Risk Management** | Identification, assessment, management methodologies; ERM integration |
| **Metrics & Targets** | GHG emissions; intensity metrics; financed emissions; targets |

#### 3.4.3 GHG Emissions Disclosure Requirements

| Emission Scope | Disclosure Requirements |
|----------------|------------------------|
| **Scope 1** | Direct emissions from owned/controlled sources - Mandatory |
| **Scope 2** | Indirect emissions from purchased energy - Mandatory |
| **Scope 3** | Other indirect emissions, including financed emissions - FY2028 |

**GHG Emissions Accounting Requirements:**
- Follow GHG Protocol Corporate Standard
- Use location-based method for Scope 2
- Apply PCAF Standard for financed emissions
- Disclose methodology and data sources

### 3.5 Third-Party Data Validation

#### 3.5.1 Data Quality Expectations

| Data Category | Validation Requirements |
|---------------|------------------------|
| **Counterparty Data** | Verify emissions data with counterparties where possible |
| **Proxy Data** | Document rationale for proxy selection and limitations |
| **Model Data** | Validate climate models and scenario data |
| **External Data** | Assess reliability of third-party data providers |

#### 3.5.2 Assurance Requirements

| Assurance Level | Application |
|-----------------|-------------|
| **Limited Assurance** | Encouraged for GHG emissions disclosures |
| **Reasonable Assurance** | May be required for larger institutions in future |
| **Internal Validation** | Required for all climate risk models and data |

### 3.6 Model Risk Management for Climate Models

#### 3.6.1 Model Governance Requirements

| Element | OSFI Expectation |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Model Inventory** | Maintain inventory of all climate risk models |
| **Model Documentation** | Document assumptions, limitations, and validation |
| **Model Validation** | Independent validation of material climate risk models |
| **Model Risk Rating** | Assign risk ratings to climate risk models |

#### 3.6.2 Climate Model Specific Considerations

| Consideration | Application |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Uncertainty** | Document and quantify model uncertainty |
| **Time Horizon** | Address challenges of long-term projections |
| **Data Quality** | Assess impact of data limitations on model outputs |
| **Expert Judgment** | Document use of expert judgment in models |

## 4. ECB Climate Risk Expectations

### 4.1 Overview

The ECB Guide on climate-related and environmental risks (November 2020, updated expectations through 2024) sets out 13 supervisory expectations for institutions under ECB direct supervision. The ECB has moved from guidance to enforcement, with binding supervisory decisions and penalties for non-compliance.

**Key Regulatory Citations:**
- ECB Guide on climate-related and environmental risks (November 2020)
- ECB Report on Good Practices for Climate Stress Testing (2022)
- ECB Thematic Review on Climate Risk (November 2022)
- EBA Guidelines on ESG Risk Management (January 2025)
- EBA Guidelines on Environmental Scenario Analysis (November 2025)

### 4.2 The 13 ECB Supervisory Expectations

| Expectation | Area | Key Requirements |
|-------------|------|------------------|
| **1** | Business Environment | Integrate C&E risks into business environment analysis |
| **2** | Business Strategy | Incorporate C&E risks into business strategy and planning |
| **3** | Business Strategy | Assess C&E risk impact on business model sustainability |
| **4** | Risk Appetite | Include C&E risks in risk appetite framework |
| **5** | Governance | Ensure board and senior management oversight of C&E risks |
| **6** | Internal Reporting | Include C&E risks in internal reporting frameworks |
| **7** | Risk Management | Integrate C&E risks into existing risk management |
| **8** | Credit Risk | Incorporate C&E risks into credit risk management |
| **9** | Other Risks | Address C&E impacts on business continuity, reputational, liability risks |
| **10** | Market Risk | Incorporate C&E risks into market risk management |
| **11** | Stress Testing | Conduct climate risk stress testing and scenario analysis |
| **12** | Liquidity Risk | Assess C&E impacts on liquidity risk and buffer calibration |
| **13** | Disclosure | Meet disclosure and regulatory reporting requirements |

### 4.3 Stress Testing Participation Requirements

#### 4.3.1 ECB Climate Risk Stress Test Framework

| Element | ECB Specification |
|---------|------------------|
| **Frequency** | At least every two years (institution-led) |
| **Horizon** | Short-term (1-3 years) and long-term (10-30 years) |
| **Scenarios** | Must include NGFS reference scenarios |
| **Risks Covered** | Physical and transition risks |
| **Portfolios** | Material exposures must be covered |

#### 4.3.2 Bottom-Up vs. Top-Down Requirements

| Approach | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **Bottom-Up** | Institutions develop own scenarios and models (preferred) |
| **Top-Down** | ECB provides scenarios (for system-wide exercises) |
| **Hybrid** | Combination of ECB scenarios with institution-specific overlays |

#### 4.3.3 Climate Stress Testing Good Practices

Per ECB 2022 Report on Good Practices:

| Practice Area | Good Practice |
|---------------|---------------|
| **Data Collection** | Engage with counterparties to collect climate-related data |
| **Scenario Design** | Use NGFS scenarios as baseline; customize for institution-specific risks |
| **Model Development** | Develop sector-specific and geographic-specific models |
| **Time Horizons** | Extend projection horizons beyond traditional stress testing |
| **Integration** | Fully integrate climate risks into traditional risk categories |
| **Validation** | Regular backtesting and benchmarking of climate models |

### 4.4 Data Quality Expectations

#### 4.4.1 Counterparty Data Requirements

| Data Category | ECB Expectations |
|---------------|------------------|
| **Emissions Data** | Collect Scope 1, 2, and 3 where available |
| **Transition Plans** | Assess quality of counterparty transition plans |
| **Physical Risk Exposure** | Geographic location of collateral and operations |
| **Sector Classification** | Detailed sector classification for risk segmentation |

#### 4.4.2 Data Gap Management

| Gap Type | Management Approach |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Missing Data** | Use proxies with documented rationale |
| **Incomplete Data** | Apply conservative assumptions |
| **Uncertain Data** | Sensitivity analysis around data uncertainty |
| **Emerging Data** | Develop processes to capture new data sources |

#### 4.4.3 Data Governance Requirements

| Element | ECB Expectation |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Data Quality Framework** | Establish climate data quality standards |
| **Data Lineage** | Document sources and transformations |
| **Data Validation** | Implement validation checks for climate data |
| **Data Storage** | Secure storage with appropriate access controls |

### 4.5 Internal Capital Adequacy Assessment

#### 4.5.1 ICAAP Climate Risk Integration

| Element | ECB Requirement |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Materiality Assessment** | Assess materiality of C&E risks for capital adequacy |
| **Quantification** | Quantify capital impact of material C&E risks |
| **Scenario Analysis** | Use scenarios to inform capital planning |
| **Management Actions** | Identify management actions to address capital impacts |
| **Documentation** | Document C&E risk considerations in ICAAP |

#### 4.5.2 Capital Planning Time Horizons

| Horizon | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| **Short-term (1-3 years)** | Near-term capital impacts from physical events |
| **Medium-term (3-10 years)** | Transition risk impacts on credit quality |
| **Long-term (10-30 years)** | Strategic capital planning for transition |

#### 4.5.3 Supervisory Review and Evaluation Process (SREP)

| SREP Element | Climate Risk Integration |
|--------------|------------------------|
| **Business Model** | Assess sustainability under climate scenarios |
| **Internal Governance** | Evaluate C&E risk governance arrangements |
| **Risk Management** | Review C&E risk management effectiveness |
| **Capital Adequacy** | Consider C&E risks in P2R and P2G determination |
| **Liquidity Adequacy** | Assess C&E impacts on liquidity position |

## 5. PRA SS3/19 Implementation

### 5.1 Overview

**Important Note:** PRA SS3/19 has been superseded by **SS5/25** (published December 2025, effective December 3, 2025). Firms must complete a gap review against updated expectations by June 3, 2026.

SS5/25 represents a significant evolution from SS3/19, shifting from high-level expectations to more concrete requirements and aligning with international standards (ISSB, BCBS, IAIS).

**Key Regulatory Citations:**
- PRA Supervisory Statement SS5/25 (December 2025)
- PRA Policy Statement PS25/25 (December 2025)
- PRA CP10/25 (Consultation Paper, May 2025)
- PRA Climate Change Adaptation Report (3rd Edition, January 2025)

### 5.2 Board Responsibility Requirements

#### 5.2.1 Enhanced Board Accountability (SS5/25)

| Responsibility | SS5/25 Requirement |
|----------------|-------------------|
| **Understanding** | Documented understanding of how climate factors affect business strategy |
| **Exposure Mapping** | Knowledge of where climate exposures are located across portfolios |
| **Metric Monitoring** | Understanding of how climate metrics, limits, and triggers are monitored |
| **Scenario Integration** | Knowledge of how scenario analysis influences decisions |
| **Risk Integration** | Climate risk integrated into routine risk assessment and strategic planning |

#### 5.2.2 Board Skills and Expertise

| Requirement | PRA Expectation |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **Climate Competency** | Board must have access to climate risk expertise |
| **Training** | Regular training on climate risk developments |
| **External Advice** | Access to external climate risk advisors where needed |
| **Challenge Function** | Ability to challenge management on climate risk matters |

#### 5.2.3 Senior Management Functions (SMF)

| SMF | Climate Risk Responsibilities |
|-----|------------------------------|
| **SMF1 - CEO** | Overall responsibility for climate risk strategy |
| **SMF4 - CFO** | Climate risk financial reporting and disclosure |
| **SMF5 - CRO** | Climate risk framework and risk appetite |
| **SMF16 - MLRO** | Climate-related financial crime considerations |

### 5.3 Risk Management Integration

#### 5.3.1 Materiality and Proportionality Framework

| Step | Requirement |
|------|-------------|

## 6. FED Climate Scenario Analysis

### 6.1 Overview

The Federal Reserve conducted a pilot Climate Scenario Analysis (CSA) exercise in 2023 with six large bank holding companies. While exploratory in nature (no capital or supervisory implications), the exercise provided significant insights into climate risk management practices and challenges.

**Key Regulatory Citations:**
- Federal Reserve Pilot Climate Scenario Analysis Exercise - Executive Summary (May 2024)
- Pilot Climate Scenario Analysis Exercise - Overview (May 2024)
- Participant Instructions (January 2023)

### 6.2 Six-Module Framework

The Federal Reserve CSA exercise was structured around two primary modules:

| Module | Description | Horizon |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **Physical Risk Module** | Estimates effect of physical hazard shocks on RRE and CRE portfolios | 1 year (2023) |
| **Transition Risk Module** | Estimates effect of transition pathways on corporate and CRE portfolios | 10 years (2023-2032) |

#### 6.2.1 Physical Risk Module Design

| Element | Specification |
|---------|-------------|
| **Scenarios** | Range of severity of shocks |
| **Hazard Types** | Common hazard (Fed-specified) + Idiosyncratic hazard (participant-chosen) |
| **Projection Horizon** | 1 year (2023) |
| **Portfolios** | Residential real estate, Commercial real estate |
| **Mitigants** | Insurance |
| **Risk Parameters** | Probability of default (PD), Loss given default (LGD) |

#### 6.2.2 Transition Risk Module Design

| Element | Specification |
|---------|-------------|
| **Scenarios** | NGFS Current Policies and Net Zero 2050 |
| **Projection Horizon** | 10 years (2023-2032) |
| **Portfolios** | Corporate loans, Commercial real estate |
| **Mitigants** | Obligor transition capacity |
| **Risk Parameters** | PD, LGD |

### 6.3 Governance and Risk Management Module

#### 6.3.1 Key Insights from Pilot Exercise

| Area | Finding |
|------|---------|
| **Approach Diversity** | Participants took different approaches driven by business models, risk views, data access |
| **Model Reliance** | Most relied on existing credit risk models with climate adjustments |
| **Data Challenges** | Significant data and modeling challenges reported |
| **Insurance Role** | Insurance highlighted as key mitigant for physical risks |
| **Uncertainty** | Climate risks viewed as highly uncertain and challenging to measure |

#### 6.3.2 Data Challenges Identified

| Challenge Category | Specific Issues |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Building Characteristics** | Lack of comprehensive data on building characteristics |
| **Insurance Coverage** | Inconsistent data on insurance coverage |
| **Counterparty Plans** | Limited information on counterparties' climate risk management plans |
| **External Dependencies** | Heavy reliance on external vendors to fill data gaps |
| **Indirect Impacts** | Difficulty assessing indirect impacts (local economy disruptions) |
| **Chronic Risks** | Challenges in modeling chronic risks (sea level rise) |

### 6.4 Scenario Selection and Implementation

#### 6.4.1 Scenario Design Elements

| Element | General Design |
|---------|---------------|
| **Risk Drivers** | Physical and transition risks modeled independently |
| **Estimation** | Loan-level estimates for select credit portfolios |
| **Balance Sheet** | Static balance sheet assumption |
| **Key Parameters** | PD and LGD |
| **As-of Date** | December 31, 2022 |

#### 6.4.2 Scenario Selection Criteria

| Criterion | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Plausibility** | Scenarios represent plausible future outcomes |
| **Range** | Cover range of potential outcomes |
| **Not Forecasts** | Scenarios are not forecasts or policy prescriptions |
| **Learning Tool** | Designed to build understanding of risk manifestation |

### 6.5 Risk Measurement and Reporting

#### 6.5.1 Key Design Choices Impacting Results

| Design Choice | Impact on Insights |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **Scope of Shocks** | Breadth of portfolio coverage affects results |
| **Scenario Severity** | Severity assumptions drive magnitude of impacts |
| **Starting Point** | As-of date affects baseline conditions |
| **Insurance Assumptions** | Insurance availability significantly impacts physical risk results |
| **Balance Sheet Assumptions** | Static vs. dynamic balance sheet affects results |

#### 6.5.2 Heterogeneity in Results

| Finding | Observation |
|---------|-------------|
| **Sector Variation** | Significant heterogeneity in impact across sectors |
| **Regional Variation** | Geographic differences in climate risk exposure |
| **Counterparty Variation** | Individual counterparty characteristics drive results |
