## 1. Basel IV Climate Risk Integration

### 1.1 Standardized Approach (SA) Modifications

#### 1.1.1 Risk Weight Adjustments for Climate-Exposed Assets

**Regulatory Basis:** BCBS FAQs on Climate-Related Financial Risks (December 2022)

**Core Requirements:**

| Risk Category | Climate Integration Requirement | Regulatory Reference |
|--------------|--------------------------------|---------------------|
| General Corporate Exposures | Evaluate climate risk impact on investment grade determination | BCBS FAQ para. 3 |
| Specialized Lending | Assess climate risk impact on "high-quality" classification | BCBS FAQ para. 4 |
| Real Estate Exposures | National supervisors must evaluate climate risk impact on risk weights | BCBS FAQ para. 5 |
| Covered Bonds | Consider issuing bank's climate risk profile | BCBS FAQ para. 3 |

**Investment Grade Assessment Requirements:**

Per BCBS FAQ (December 2022), banks must:
1. **Evaluate material climate-related risks** under adverse conditions when determining investment grade status
2. **Assess capacity** of corporates to meet financial commitments considering climate impacts
3. **Implement systematic credit review processes** to identify early credit quality deterioration
4. **Document climate risk considerations** in credit assessments where external ratings are not used

**Risk Weight Adjustments for Climate-Exposed Sectors:**

```
Climate Risk Adjustment Formula (Conceptual):
Adjusted RW = Base RW × (1 + Climate Risk Factor)

Where Climate Risk Factor considers:
- Sectoral transition risk exposure (0-25%)
- Physical risk geographic concentration (0-15%)
- Counterparty climate vulnerability score (0-10%)
```

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Climate risk assessment methodology must be documented in credit policies
- Sector-specific climate risk factors must be justified with data
- Review frequency: minimum annually
- Board approval required for material methodology changes

#### 1.1.2 Real Estate Collateral Haircuts

**Regulatory Requirements:**

Per BCBS FAQ para. 5 and ECB Guide on Climate-Related Risks:

| Aspect | Regulatory Expectation |
|--------|----------------------|
| Market Value Assessment | Determine if current market value incorporates climate risk impacts |
| Weather-Related Hazards | Explicit consideration in collateral valuation |
| Regulatory/Political Transition Risks | Assessment of policy impact on property values |
| Consumer Behavior Changes | Consideration of shifting demand patterns |
| Haircut Adjustments | Additional margin for climate-exposed collateral |

**Collateral Valuation Climate Risk Factors:**

1. **Physical Risk Factors:**
   - Flood zone exposure (FEMA/ equivalent mapping)
   - Wildfire risk scores
   - Coastal erosion projections
   - Heat stress indicators
   - Water scarcity assessments

2. **Transition Risk Factors:**
   - Energy efficiency ratings (EPC scores)
   - Stranded asset risk (fossil fuel dependency)
   - Regulatory compliance costs (retrofit requirements)
   - Carbon pricing exposure

**Haircut Calculation Framework:**

```
Climate-Adjusted Haircut = Base Haircut + Climate Risk Premium

Climate Risk Premium Components:
- Physical risk surcharge: 0-10% (based on hazard mapping)
- Transition risk surcharge: 0-5% (based on energy efficiency)
- Concentration risk surcharge: 0-3% (portfolio-level)
```

**EBA Proposed Requirements (October 2023):**
- Incorporate environmental risks into immovable property collateral valuation
- Apply scenario analysis to collateral value projections
- Document climate risk adjustments in collateral policies

#### 1.1.3 Corporate Exposure Adjustments

**Due Diligence Requirements:**

Per BCBS FAQ para. 3 and ECB Guide Expectation 7.4:

| Due Diligence Element | Climate Risk Integration |
|----------------------|-------------------------|
| Client Onboarding | Climate vulnerability assessment mandatory |
| Ongoing Monitoring | Periodic climate risk review |
| Sector Analysis | Climate risk materiality by sector |
| Geographic Assessment | Location-specific physical risk evaluation |
| Transition Plan Review | Assessment of borrower's climate strategy |

**Supervisory Slotting Criteria for Specialized Lending:**

Banks must analyze how climate risks impact:
- Financial strength (revenue/cost impacts)
- Political and legal risks (regulatory changes)
- Physical risks (asset damage, disruption)
- Asset characteristics (location, resilience)
- Risk mitigation measures (insurance, adaptation)

### 1.2 IRB Approach Modifications

#### 1.2.1 PD/LGD Estimation Requirements

**Regulatory Basis:** BCBS FAQ on IRB Approach (December 2022)

**PD Estimation Requirements:**

| Element | Climate Integration Requirement |
|---------|-------------------------------|
| Rating Assignment | Consider climate risk impact on obligor performance |
| Data Collection | Granular level data on climate risk factors |
| External Rating Mapping | Assess if scale reflects material climate risks |
| Model Evaluation | Critical evaluation of data sources, granularity, historical time series |
| Conservative Adjustments | Required where external ratings don't reflect climate risk |

**LGD Estimation Requirements:**

```
Climate-Adjusted LGD = f(Base LGD, Climate Risk Factors)

Climate Risk Factors:
- Collateral climate vulnerability
- Recovery rate climate impacts
- Downturn climate scenario effects
- Insurance coverage climate risks
```

**Specific Requirements:**
1. **Facility Rating Considerations:** Material information on climate risk impact on facility characteristics
2. **Data Maintenance:** Effective process for climate-relevant data collection
3. **Conservative Approach:** Where information is insufficient, apply margin of conservatism
4. **Margin of Conservatism:** Additional buffer for data deficiencies and climate uncertainty

#### 1.2.2 Long-Run Average Adjustments

**Calculation Requirements:**

Per BCBS FAQ, banks with portfolios materially exposed to climate risks must:

1. **Consider climate risks directly** in PD/LGD/EAD estimates
2. **Add margin of error** to reflect data deficiencies
3. **Address challenges:**
   - Range of impact uncertainties
   - Limitations in historical data relevance
   - Time horizon questions
   - Incomplete methodology issues

**Long-Run Average Formula Adjustments:**

```
Climate-Adjusted Long-Run Average = 
    Historical Average × Climate Risk Multiplier + Margin of Conservatism

Climate Risk Multiplier Range: 1.0 - 1.25
Margin of Conservatism: 5-15% (based on data quality)
```

### 1.3 Output Floor Implications

#### 1.3.1 Climate Risk in Standardized Approach

**Output Floor Calculation Framework:**

Per Basel III Finalization (Basel IV):

```
Output Floor = 72.5% × Standardized Approach RWA

Climate Risk Considerations in SA:
- Risk weight adjustments for climate-exposed sectors
- Collateral haircut adjustments for physical risk
- Corporate exposure rating adjustments
```

**Implementation Timeline:**
- 2023: 50% floor
- 2024: 55% floor
- 2025: 60% floor
- 2026: 65% floor
- 2028: 72.5% floor (final)

#### 1.3.2 Floor Calculation Methodology

**Climate Risk Integration Steps:**

1. **Identify Climate-Exposed Exposures:**
   - High carbon intensity sectors
   - Physical risk geographic concentrations
   - Transition-vulnerable counterparties

2. **Apply Climate Risk Adjustments:**
   - Sector-specific risk weight add-ons
   - Geographic risk multipliers
   - Counterparty climate scores

3. **Calculate Floor-Adjusted RWA:**
   ```
   Floor-Adjusted RWA = max(IRB RWA, 72.5% × SA RWA with Climate Adjustments)
   ```

## 2. ICAAP Regulatory Expectations

### 2.1 ECB Expectations for Climate ICAAP

#### 2.1.1 Materiality Assessment Requirements

**Regulatory Basis:** ECB Guide on Climate-Related and Environmental Risks (2020, updated 2022)

**Materiality Assessment Framework:**

| Assessment Dimension | ECB Expectation |
|---------------------|-----------------|
| Risk Identification | Comprehensive inventory of climate risk exposures |
| Quantitative Assessment | Monetary impact estimation where possible |
| Qualitative Assessment | Narrative description of non-quantifiable risks |
| Time Horizons | Short (1-3 years), Medium (3-10 years), Long (10-30 years) |
| Review Frequency | Annual minimum, triggered reviews for material changes |

**Materiality Thresholds:**

```
Quantitative Materiality Thresholds:
- Individual exposure: >0.5% of Tier 1 capital
- Aggregate exposure: >1% of Tier 1 capital
- Sector concentration: >5% of total exposures

Qualitative Materiality Indicators:
- Strategic impact on business model
- Reputational risk exposure
- Regulatory compliance risk
- Litigation risk potential
```

**ECB Deadline:** Full integration of climate risks into ICAAP by end of 2024

#### 2.1.2 Methodology Documentation

**Required Documentation Elements:**

| Element | Documentation Requirement |
|---------|--------------------------|

| Quantification Approaches | Models, assumptions, data sources |

| Aggregation Methodology | Approach for combining risks across categories |
| Capital Allocation | Methodology for assigning capital to climate risks |
| Sensitivity Analysis | Assessment of key assumption impacts |

**ECB Observed Practice (Box 7):**

One bank applies environmental ratings to financed assets:

#### 4.1.2 Scenario Probability Weighting

**Probability Weighting Framework:**

```
Weighted ECL = Σ (ECL_scenario_i × Probability_i)

Where:
- ECL_scenario_i = ECL under scenario i
- Probability_i = Assigned probability of scenario i
- Σ Probability_i = 1.0

Climate Scenario Considerations:
- Base case: 40-60% weighting
- Upside case: 10-20% weighting
- Downside case: 20-30% weighting
- Tail risk: 5-10% weighting
```
