## 1. Persona

**Name:** (Composite — representative of the role)
**Title:** Senior Associate / Manager — Sustainable Energy Finance (Developer-Lender Liaison)
**Organisation type:** Renewable energy developer, project finance advisory firm, or DFI (Development Finance Institution) operating in SEA
**Location:** Singapore or Manila (primary); project sites across Philippines, Indonesia, Vietnam, Thailand
**Reports to:** Head of Project Finance / Chief Investment Officer

**Role Definition:**
This persona sits at the critical intersection between energy developers (who build projects) and financiers (who fund them). They do not solely model financial returns, nor solely manage construction. They make renewable energy transactions bankable — translating technical project parameters into the risk language understood by lenders, and translating lender requirements into constraints developers must meet. They identify commercially viable opportunities, quantify the risks, structure the financing, and drive transactions to financial close.

**Day-to-Day Responsibilities:**
- Screen new renewable energy project opportunities (solar, wind, BESS, geothermal, hydro, green hydrogen) against bankability and financial return thresholds
- Build project finance models (DSCR, IRR, LCOE, NPV) for lender due diligence packages
- Conduct taxonomy classification under Singapore-Asia Taxonomy (SAT), ASEAN Taxonomy, and lender-specific ESG frameworks
- Assess carbon credit eligibility and model energy transition credit (ETC) revenues alongside power purchase agreement (PPA) revenues
- Prepare TNFD LEAP assessments for projects in biodiversity-sensitive areas (geothermal, hydro)
- Structure and execute green bonds, sustainability-linked loans, and blended finance instruments
- Coordinate with coal phase-out transactions (ADB ETM, JETP country platforms)
- Support Just Transition planning for communities affected by coal plant retirement

**Primary KPIs:**
| KPI | Typical Benchmark | Notes |
|-----|-------------------|-------|
| Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) | 1.25x–1.40x minimum | Lender covenant; below 1.25x = non-bankable |
| Equity IRR | 11–15% (solar/wind SEA) | Higher for geothermal (15–18%) and early-stage |
| LCOE — Solar (SEA) | USD 30–60/MWh | Country-dependent; Philippines ~USD 45–55/MWh |
| LCOE — Onshore Wind (SEA) | USD 50–80/MWh | Higher due to grid constraints in archipelagos |

| TNFD LEAP completion | Required for hydro, geothermal | Nature-sensitive project finance requirement |
| Paris Alignment | IEA NZE 2050 glidepath | Required for NZBA/CA100+ bank lenders |

## 2. Context: The SEA Energy Transition Opportunity

Southeast Asia is in the midst of a major energy transition. The region has among the highest solar irradiance globally, rapidly falling renewable LCOE, and national commitments under NDCs and JETP frameworks (Indonesia US$20B, Vietnam US$15.5B) — but faces structural barriers to project finance.

**Key market characteristics:**
- **Philippines:** 35% renewable energy target by 2030 under REIPPA; 13GW solar pipeline; ADB ETM first completed transaction (ACEN SLTEC 246 MW, 2024)
- **Indonesia:** 23% renewable target by 2025 (missed); JETP US$20B; PLN coal plant retirement pipeline (Cirebon-1, 660 MW, in progress under ETM); captive power replacement opportunity
- **Vietnam:** 25GW offshore wind target by 2030; transition from FiT to competitive auction (DPPA market); significant grid constraint risk
- **Thailand:** 30% renewables by 2037 (PDP 2024); strong private sector participation; green bond market developing
- **Singapore:** Regional hub for project finance; MAS SLGS grant scheme (world's first sustainable loan grant, SGD 15M, Dec 2028 expiry); SAT provides world-first multi-sector transition taxonomy

**Critical finance challenge:** Lenders in the region require investor-grade risk assessment packages. Most renewable developers lack the internal capacity to produce PCAF-aligned financed emissions calculations, NGFS scenario analysis, TNFD LEAP assessments, and SAT taxonomy certificates — the requirements imposed by NZBA-member banks providing project finance. This is the gap this persona fills.

**Key frameworks this persona operates under:**
| Framework | Relevance |
|-----------|-----------|
| Singapore-Asia Taxonomy (SAT) | World's first multi-sector transition taxonomy; Green / Amber / Transition / Red; coal phase-out criteria (by 2040, max 25 years, pre-Dec 2021 FID); MAS-endorsed |
| ADB Energy Transition Mechanism (ETM) | Blended finance for early coal retirement; ACEN SLTEC Philippines world's first; Cirebon-1 Indonesia in progress |
| ASEAN Taxonomy v2 (2023) | Regional taxonomy; Plus Standard (Green), Transitional Finance (Amber), Watch List (Red) |
| MAS Sustainable Loan Grant Scheme (SLGS) | World's first; covers SPO, framework development costs; up to SGD 100K per loan; runs to Dec 2028 |
| IRENA Five Pillars of Bankability | Financial modelling, offtake security, developer track record, technical readiness, Paris alignment |
| Verra CCCI (ETC methodology) | Energy Transition Credits; approved May 2025; 2% net carbon revenue for just transition |
| NGFS Phase V (Nov 2024) | Central bank scenario standard; GDP loss 2–4× previous estimates; used by all NZBA lenders |
| TNFD LEAP | Locate, Evaluate, Assess, Prepare — nature risk framework for project finance |

### 4.1 Power Plant Assessment — `/sector-assessments` → Power Plants Panel

**What the energy expert needs:**
Technical-financial assessment of a proposed renewable project or an existing coal plant being retired. For new projects: LCOE, capacity factor analysis, DSCR calculation. For coal retirement: remaining economic life, stranding date, NPV of early retirement.

**How the platform is used:**

**New renewable project (e.g., 100 MW solar + BESS in Luzon, Philippines):**

The analyst opens `/sector-assessments` → Power Plant Assessment. They input:
- Plant type: Solar PV + BESS (4-hour)
- Installed capacity: 100 MW solar + 50 MW / 200 MWh BESS
- Capacity factor: 19.5% (Philippines solar irradiance, P90 estimate)
- CAPEX: USD 85M (solar) + USD 60M (BESS) = USD 145M total
- OPEX: USD 2.5M/year
- Offtake: 20-year PPA at USD 58/MWh (signed with industrial off-taker)
- Fuel cost: zero (solar); BESS cycling cost USD 8/MWh
- Carbon price assumption: NGFS Net Zero 2050 (USD 25/tCO₂ in 2026, rising to USD 130 by 2035) for ETC revenue modelling

Platform outputs:
- **LCOE: USD 47.3/MWh** — below PPA price, positive margin confirmed
- **Project DSCR (Year 1–5 average): 1.31x** — above 1.25x minimum; bankable
- **Equity IRR: 13.2%** — within the 11–15% target range for the market
- **ETC revenue stream**: at NGFS carbon pricing, ETCs generate USD 1.8M/year additional revenue (modelled as 2% of net carbon avoided per CCCI methodology) — improves DSCR to 1.38x and IRR to 14.1%
- **Stranding stress test**: under NGFS Current Policies (no carbon price), DSCR drops to 1.22x — below minimum; risk flagged

**Coal plant retirement (ETM transaction modelling):**

For the Cirebon-1 equivalent (660 MW, Indonesia), the analyst inputs the existing coal plant parameters. The platform calculates:
- **Financial stranding date under NGFS Net Zero 2050**: 2031 (LCOE + carbon cost > market price)
- **Residual asset value at stranding date**: USD 280M (discounted remaining book value)
- **ETM compensation required**: USD 280M – USD 120M (scrap/residual) = USD 160M blended finance requirement
- **Just transition cost estimate**: 12% of ETM compensation (staff transition, community programmes)

This is the exact calculation needed to structure an ADB ETM transaction.

### 4.2 Carbon Calculator — `/carbon`

**What the energy expert needs:**
Model carbon credit eligibility and revenue for a renewable energy project — both CDM/VCS legacy credits and the new Energy Transition Credits (ETCs) under Verra CCCI methodology.

**How the platform is used:**

The analyst opens `/carbon` and selects the Carbon Calculator v2 (Scope 1/2/3 + CDM/VCS/CBAM). They input:
- Project type: Grid-connected solar PV
- Baseline grid emission factor: 0.6985 tCO₂/MWh (Philippines DOE, 2024)
- Annual generation: 191 GWh/year (100 MW × 19.5% CF × 8,760 hours × 10% degradation adj.)
- Additionality: confirmed (no equivalent solar at this scale without carbon finance)
- Monitoring approach: IRMA methodology (metered generation)

Platform calculates:
- **Annual GHG reduction**: 191 GWh × 0.6985 tCO₂/MWh = **133,600 tCO₂e/year**
- **20-year carbon credit pool**: ~2.4 MtCO₂e (with 10% buffer pool set aside per VCS)
- **At CER/VER price USD 15/tCO₂**: USD 1.9M/year carbon revenue
- **At ETC pricing (linked to avoided grid emissions × 2% CCCI formula)**: USD 1.1M/year
- **CDM vs. VCS vs. ETC comparison table**: platform shows net revenue under each mechanism — allowing the analyst to select the optimal structure

For lenders, the platform produces the carbon credit revenue assumption that feeds directly into the debt service model in the Power Plant Assessment module.

### 4.3 Scenario Analysis — `/scenario-analysis`

**What the energy expert needs:**
Run stress tests on the project under NGFS scenarios — specifically testing whether project returns remain viable under different carbon pricing trajectories and physical climate risk profiles (typhoon frequency, solar irradiance variability).

**How the platform is used:**

The analyst opens `/scenario-analysis`. They select the 100 MW solar + BESS project and run three scenarios:

**Net Zero 2050 (Orderly):**
- Carbon price rises USD 25 → USD 130/tCO₂ by 2035. ETC revenues increase; project economics improve over time.
- Physical risk: low chronic physical risk; moderate extreme weather. Philippines typhoon risk modelled — P99 generation loss 12% in worst years.
- DSCR in all years: above 1.25x. **Scenario status: PASS**

**Delayed Transition (Disorderly):**
- Low carbon price until 2030, then sudden shock (USD 150/tCO₂ by 2032). Carbon revenue front-loaded under ETCs reduces but then spikes.
- Grid competition risk: coal plants maintain economics until 2030, suppressing electricity prices. PPA provides insulation, but merchant price floor assumption of USD 40/MWh under stress tests DSCR.
- Minimum DSCR: 1.19x in 2029–2030. **Scenario status: FLAG** — lender covenant breach in 2 years; needs cash reserve mechanism

**Current Policies (Hot House World):**
- No carbon price. ETC revenues absent. Physical risk high — chronic temperature increase reduces solar output by 3% by 2040.
- DSCR in 2038–2045: 1.09x. **Scenario status: FAIL** — requires project redesign or additional credit enhancement

The analyst presents the Delayed Transition flag to the project developer and proposes a 6-month Debt Service Reserve Account (DSRA) to cure the 2029–2030 DSCR breach. The platform's What-If studio allows them to test the DSRA sizing (USD 8.5M) and confirm DSCR is restored to 1.26x minimum under all scenarios.

- Input: installed capacity (248 MW), fuel type (thermal coal), heat rate (10.2 GJ/MWh), commissioning year (2002), outstanding debt (USD 85M, maturity 2029)

For an ETM package, the analyst takes these numbers directly to the ADB and blended finance structuring team as the basis for concessional finance sizing.

### 4.5 Nature Risk — TNFD LEAP — `/nature-risk`

**What the energy expert needs:**
For hydro and geothermal projects in biodiversity-sensitive areas, NZBA lenders increasingly require a TNFD LEAP assessment. This is also required for projects seeking IFC Performance Standards compliance (necessary for DFI lending by ADB, IFC, AIIB).

**How the platform is used:**

For a 50 MW run-of-river hydro project in North Luzon, Philippines (near the Cordillera region):

**Locate:** The analyst inputs project GPS coordinates. The platform's Nature Risk module overlays:
- Protected area proximity (within 5 km of NIPAS protected area — flagged)
- Watershed risk (upstream Cagayan River basin — critical freshwater basin)
- Biodiversity intactness index: 0.62 (moderate — fragmented forest)

**Evaluate:** The platform runs the ENCORE dependency screening for hydropower:
- Water regulation: HIGH dependency
- Soil and sediment retention: HIGH dependency
- Climate regulation: MEDIUM dependency
- Freshwater provisioning: HIGH dependency

**Assess:** TNFD materiality assessment:
- River flow variability under NGFS 2°C scenario: +22% annual variation by 2040 (linked to scenario data)
- Fish species at risk from flow diversion: 4 endemic species (IBA-flagged)
- Sedimentation risk during construction: HIGH — mitigation plan required

**Prepare:** The platform generates the TNFD LEAP output report, suitable for inclusion in the lender due diligence package. Recommended disclosures:
- Biodiversity monitoring commitment (upstream + downstream)
- Flow release schedule for endemic fish species
- Sedimentation management plan (IFC PS6 compliant)
- Quarterly nature-related risk reporting during construction

This output replaces a USD 80,000–150,000 third-party TNFD consultant report for standard projects.

### 4.6 Regulatory — SAT Taxonomy Classification — `/regulatory`

**What the energy expert needs:**
Classify a proposed transaction under the Singapore-Asia Taxonomy (SAT) and/or ASEAN Taxonomy to qualify for green/sustainable finance. SAT classification is a prerequisite for MAS SLGS grant eligibility and for green bond issuance under SGX-listed green bond frameworks.

**How the platform is used:**

The analyst opens `/regulatory` → Singapore-Asia Taxonomy panel. They input the transaction parameters:
- Transaction type: Project finance (solar PV + BESS)
- Sector: Energy (Power generation)
- Technology: Solar PV, Grid storage (BESS)
- Geography: Philippines (non-Singapore, but SAT applies for Singapore-based lender)

Platform classifies:
- **SAT Traffic Light: GREEN** — Solar PV is unconditionally Green under SAT Power sector criteria (no threshold required for utility-scale solar)
- **BESS classification: GREEN** — Grid-scale battery storage classified Green under SAT (enables renewable integration)
- **Do No Significant Harm (DNSH) screening**: Water use (low — dry-cooling), Biodiversity (standard agricultural land — DNSH met), Circular economy (panel recycling plan required — input required from developer)

For a coal retrofit transaction:
- Coal plant: **RED** — SAT excludes new coal regardless of age
- Coal ETM (early retirement + replacement): **AMBER / Transition** — SAT has specific criteria for coal phase-out (must close by 2040, max 25 years remaining life, pre-Dec 2021 FID; must commit to just transition plan). Platform validates each criterion and assigns GREEN/AMBER/RED status per criterion.

The SAT classification certificate produced by the platform is submitted to the lender as part of the green/sustainable finance due diligence package and supports MAS SLGS grant application.

### 4.7 Supply Chain — Scope 3 for Project Finance — `/supply-chain`

**What the energy expert needs:**
For construction-phase projects, lenders and off-takers increasingly require Scope 3 supply chain emissions disclosure — particularly for solar module supply chains (China manufacturing risk) and BESS battery supply chains (cobalt, lithium sourcing).

**How the platform is used:**

The analyst opens `/supply-chain`. For the 100 MW solar project:
- Input: 250,000 solar modules (400 Wp, monocrystalline), supplier: tier-1 Chinese manufacturer
- BESS: 200 MWh LFP battery, supplier: CATL (Chinese manufacturer)

Platform calculates Scope 3 Category 1 (purchased goods) embedded emissions:
- **Solar modules**: ~150 kg CO₂e/kWp × 100,000 kWp = **15,000 tCO₂e** (manufacturing emissions; China grid intensity ~530 gCO₂/kWh; 2.5 kWh/Wp manufacturing energy)
- **BESS**: ~75 kg CO₂e/kWh × 200,000 kWh = **15,000 tCO₂e** (LFP battery manufacturing)
- **Total construction-phase Scope 3**: ~32,000 tCO₂e (incl. steel, cabling, civil works)

Compared to lifetime avoided emissions (133,600 tCO₂e/year × 25 years = 3.34 MtCO₂e), the embedded carbon payback period is **0.24 years (< 3 months)**. This strengthens the carbon credit additionality case.

**Supply chain sustainability flags:**
- Solar module supplier: InfluenceMap score D (moderate negative climate lobbying) — disclosed in lender ESG report
- BESS supplier: Xinjiang polysilicon supply chain risk flagged (Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act relevance for US-linked investors) — documented in supply chain DD

### 4.8 Financial Risk — PCAF Lender Portfolio — `/financial-risk`

**What the energy expert needs:**
NZBA-member banks providing project finance now require PCAF-aligned financed emissions data from borrowers as part of their portfolio reporting. The energy expert must provide this data to the lender as part of the financing package. This also applies to equity investors (infrastructure funds, pension funds) requiring PCAF Category 7 (project finance) attribution.

**How the platform is used:**

The analyst opens `/financial-risk` → PCAF Panel. They create a PCAF record for the project as a borrower entity:
- Entity type: SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle) — solar project company
- Outstanding debt: USD 95M (senior debt, 20-year tenor)
- Borrower Scope 1 emissions: zero (operating solar, no combustion)
- Borrower Scope 2 emissions: minimal (site operations, grid-connected)
- Attribution factor: USD 95M (DBS loan) / USD 145M (total project value) = 0.655
- Financed emissions attributed to lender: 0.655 × 0 tCO₂e/year (operating emissions) = zero

**PCAF DQS (Data Quality Score)**: Score 1 — project-specific, directly measured metered generation data. This is the highest quality score under PCAF and supports lender's portfolio-level PCAF reporting.

For coal plant retirement, the platform calculates the financed emissions from the REMAINING operating life — attributing the coal plant's Scope 1 combustion emissions to the lender's portfolio proportionally, then models the elimination of those emissions on the retirement date.

## 6. End-to-End Scenario: The 100 MW Philippines Solar + BESS Transaction

**Context:** An energy developer has a signed 20-year PPA at USD 58/MWh with an industrial off-taker in Luzon. They have land (DOE RA 11371 approved area), grid connection offer (NGCP), and EPC contractor shortlisted (SPNEC / ACEN subcontractor). They need to reach financial close with a consortium of 3 lenders (DBS, BDO, ADB private sector) within 5 months. The energy expert is engaged to produce the lender due diligence package.

**Week 1 — Project Screening and Initial Assessment**
The expert opens `/sector-assessments` → Power Plant panel. They input all project parameters. The platform computes LCOE (USD 47.3/MWh), DSCR (1.31x), and Equity IRR (13.2%). Project clears all primary bankability thresholds. ETC revenue modelled at USD 1.8M/year. Recommendation: proceed to full due diligence.
